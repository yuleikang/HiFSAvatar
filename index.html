HiFSAvatar:High-Fidelity and Stylized Gaussian Head Avatars with Geometry-Aware Enhancement

Yulei Kang, Jian-Fang Hu, Tianming Liang, Beihao Xia, Jianguo Zhang, Wei-Shi Zheng
Sun Yat-Sen University
Code will be released soon

Abstract

Reconstructing photorealistic and animatable 3D head avatars from monocular image sequences is a valuable yet highly challenging task. The primary difficulty lies in capturing both generic head deformations caused by variations in expression and pose, and non-generic head changes, such as hair and glasses, which require accurate modeling of individual attributes. While previous methods achieve impressive results by integrating 3D Gaussian Splatting with a parametric head model, the quality of head reconstruction is still suffer from the inaccurate geometric deformations and the lack of local geometry-aware modeling. Moreover, these methods cannot perform style transfer on discrete Gaussian primitives of head avatars, limiting the potential application of this task. To address these challenges, we propose HiFSAvatar, a novel framework that reconstructs high-fidelity, stylized 3D Gaussian head avatars through geometry-aware enhancement. HiFSAvatar employs learnable personalized blendshapes and features to improve the flexibility and accuracy of geometric deformations. Furthermore, we introduce an anchor-driven strategy for local geometric optimization of Gaussian attributes, which extracts local geometric cues via a local feature aggregation network to enhance dynamic head details. To achieve stylization, we propose a color distribution and style feature alignment strategy, ensuring both faithful style transfer and geometric realism. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our geometry-aware modeling approach, which significantly enhances the reconstruction quality and outperforms previous methods in both accuracy and stylization.

Method

Overview of the proposed HiFSAvatar: Given an input image, FLAME tracking first estimates the pose $\theta$, expression $\psi$, and shape $\beta$ parameters before training. These parameters are then refined using a learnable feature $f_{pg}$, and fed into a geometry deformation process that optimized with learnable shape offsets ($\Delta X_S$) and blendshape bases ($\Delta P$, $\Delta E$) to generate the deformed mesh $X_P$. Based on $X_P$, we compute the geometric factors ($\mathbf{n}_f$, $\mathbf{s}_f$, $\mathbf{r}_f$), and initialize the Gaussians on $X_P$ using a predefined UV mapping function $\mathcal{F}_{uv}$, including position ${\mu}_d$, rotation $\mathbf{r}_d$, scale $\mathbf{s}_d$, opacity $\mathbf{o}$, and color $\mathbf{c}$. The geometric attributes of the Gaussians are further refined through the Anchor-driven Local Geometric Enhancement module, and the final render is obtained via differentiable rasterization. To achieve stylization, the color attribute is further optimized to adapt to different styles.
